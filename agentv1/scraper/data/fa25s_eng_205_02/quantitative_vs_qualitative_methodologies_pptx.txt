--- Slide 1 --- # Quantitative vs. Qualitative Methodologies
- Quantitative vs. Qualitative Methodologies
- Comparison of Two Major Research Approaches
 --- Slide 2 --- # Definition Overview
- Definition Overview
- Quantitative: Numerical, measurable, hypothesis-driven research.
- Qualitative: Descriptive, exploratory, meaning-based research.
 --- Slide 3 --- # Quantitative Methodology
- Quantitative Methodology
- Deductive approach: Theory → Hypothesis → Test
- Data: Numbers, closed-ended questions
- Methods: Surveys, experiments, structured observations
- Analysis: Statistical tests (e.g., regression, ANOVA)
- Strengths: Objectivity, generalizability, replicability
 --- Slide 4 --- # Qualitative Methodology
- Qualitative Methodology
- Inductive approach: Observation → Patterns → Theory
- Data: Words, open-ended responses
- Methods: Interviews, focus groups, ethnography
- Analysis: Thematic, narrative, content analysis
- Strengths: Rich detail, contextual understanding, meaning-making
 --- Slide 5 --- # Key Differences
- Key Differences
- Quantitative: Focus on 'how much', 'how many'
- Qualitative: Focus on 'how', 'why', 'what is the meaning'
- Quantitative: Large samples; Qualitative: Small, purposive samples
- Quantitative: Statistical analysis; Qualitative: Interpretive analysis
 --- Slide 6 --- # Mixed Methods
- Mixed Methods
- Combines both quantitative and qualitative approaches.
- Example: Surveys for general trends + Interviews for deeper insight.
